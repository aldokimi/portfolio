# About this website

This is **Mohammed Al-Dokimi’s personal portfolio** — a single website that introduces who he is, what he does, and how to get in touch. It is designed to feel like a calm, modern “control panel” rather than a stiff PDF resume.

## What you can do here

### Explore the career story (home page)

The main page is split into simple sections you can switch between:

- **Overview** — A short intro, a playful terminal-style welcome, and a visual map of career topics (work, certifications, skills, education, projects).
- **Experience** — Past and current jobs, with plain-language summaries.
- **Skills** — Tools and topics he works with, grouped by area.
- **Certs** — Professional certifications (especially Red Hat), with details when you want them.
- **Education** — Schools and degrees, including an interactive view of university subjects.
- **Projects** — Side projects and open work, with links where relevant.

You do not need to install anything. Everything runs in the browser.

### Read articles (Logs)

Under **Logs**, you’ll find blog-style articles — notes on engineering, learning, or anything he chooses to publish. New articles can be added without rebuilding the whole site.

### Try the terminal (optional fun)

**Terminal** is an interactive page styled like a command line. It is mostly for atmosphere and exploration; it is not required to understand the rest of the site.

### Get in touch (Contact)

The **Contact** page lists ways to reach him (email, LinkedIn, GitHub, phone) and describes **services** he may offer, such as technical consulting, career coaching, and IT project help.

## For Mohammed (the site owner)

- **Public pages** — CV content lives in the project and updates when the site is redeployed.
- **Blog posts** — Written and published through a private **Admin** area (only for him). In production, that area is locked behind Cloudflare login so visitors cannot access it.
- **Hosting** — The site runs on Cloudflare (fast, global delivery). Blog posts are stored in a small cloud database so publishing an article does not require a full site rebuild.

## Who it is for

- **Recruiters and colleagues** — Quick picture of background, skills, and certifications.
- **Potential clients** — Services and contact options on one page.
- **Anyone curious** — Optional articles on the Logs page.

## Privacy and safety

- The contact page only shows links and information meant to be public.
- The admin/editor area is not open to the public in production.
